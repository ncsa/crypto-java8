Working with X.509 Certificates

X.509 certs are a standard feature of the web. It is past this blurb to give you a tutorial on them, but this will show you how to use the Crypt8 suite of tools to generate them.

Lifecycle

The normal lifecycle consists of making

  • A public/private keypair
  • A certification request (PKCS 10)
  • Requesting the certificate from a certificate authority (CA) by sending the PEM encoded PKCS 10 request.
  • Interpreting the response from the CA, which may be a single certificate or a chain of them.

You must store the key pair for later use since the certificate(s) will be useless without them.
